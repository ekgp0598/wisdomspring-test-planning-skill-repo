# Visual Design Summary

Use this file when the user asks what WisdomSpring looks like, how the product feels, or how screens are visually structured.

This summary is derived from the bundled design images under `references/design-images/`.

## Overall tone

The service has a calm editorial look built from:

- soft sage and olive greens
- warm grays and off-white backgrounds
- muted accent pills and badges
- card-heavy layouts
- large content imagery with literary and classical references

It feels more like a premium humanities magazine or reflective study club than a typical corporate LMS.

## Visual identity

- The top navigation stays minimal with logo, search, and menu.
- Most screens rely on generous white space.
- Cards have rounded corners and soft shadows or soft borders rather than sharp enterprise tables.
- Green is used as the primary action color, status color, and category highlight.
- Yellow or orange appears sparingly for `NEW` or emphasis badges.

## Home screen impression

From the home images:

- the page opens with a reflective question banner
- theme cards use strong book-cover or portrait visuals
- recommendation areas are stacked as horizontal content rails
- quote cards and answer rails break up the feed with softer blocks

The home screen communicates that this is a discovery-driven service with editorial curation, not a dense dashboard.

## Question feed impression

From the question-feed images:

- answers are shown in long vertical cards
- the current question is visually pinned at the top
- answer cards use pale green backgrounds and quiet separators
- interaction controls exist, but they are understated

This makes the community feel moderated and thoughtful instead of loud or gamified.

## Theme detail impression

From the theme-detail images:

- the upper area introduces the theme with a strong hero image and summary
- progress, content list, and expandable sections are visible
- content entries resemble an editorial playlist more than a lesson table
- cards and accordion sections guide the user down the page

The structure suggests a curated reading or viewing path.

## Profile impression

From the profile images:

- the user's record is framed like a personal archive
- counters and tabs are compact and secondary
- question-answer history remains the visual center
- controls for editing or filtering do not dominate the screen

The profile feels like a reflective logbook, not a social profile-first product.

## Membership impression

From the membership images:

- subscription state is summarized in a large status card
- price and period are visible at a glance
- actions such as cancellation are available but visually subdued
- the layout is intentionally simple and trust-oriented

This suggests that billing is necessary but not meant to overpower the learning experience.

## FAQ and support impression

From the FAQ image:

- categories are tabbed across the top
- accordion questions dominate the page
- icons and spacing stay simple
- the screen prioritizes clarity over branding flourish

## Mobile versus web

- Mobile screens feel feed-like and vertically continuous.
- Web screens keep the same quiet visual language but add more whitespace and wider card grids.
- The service preserves the same emotional tone across platforms rather than redesigning by platform.

## QA implications from design

- Quiet styling means state cues such as active, selected, disabled, and hidden must remain sufficiently visible.
- Because many cards share similar colors, hierarchy and status labels are critical.
- Since the experience depends on editorial calm, clutter, broken spacing, or inconsistent badge treatment can feel more severe than in louder products.
